## How to Maximize Your ISS Tracking Experience  

### 1. Pin the App to Your Home Screen  

Adding the tracker to your home screen creates an always‑available dashboard. You can set a widget that shows the next pass, the station’s current location, and even a quick‑access button for the time simulator.  

### 2. Adjust Refresh Intervals for Battery Life  

If you’re on a long flight or a hike, set the refresh interval to 60 seconds or more. For a quick glance, a 5‑second refresh is handy but drains battery faster.  

### 3. Use the “Night Mode” When Watching the Sky  

The app’s day/night visualization syncs with your local sunrise/sunset. Switching to night mode highlights the ISS’s path against the darkened Earth, making it easier to spot when it’s overhead.  

### 4. Leverage the Pass‑Prediction Feature  

Enter your exact coordinates (or allow the app to auto‑detect them) and you’ll receive a list of upcoming passes, complete with start time, end time, elevation angle, and visibility duration. Mark the passes that fit your schedule on a calendar or set a notification.  

### 5. Play the Time Simulator  

Want to see how the ISS traveled during a past launch? Use the simulator to rewind or fast‑forward to any date. This is especially useful for students or hobbyists wanting to visualize orbital mechanics without complex software.  

### 6. Share Crew Information with Friends  

The crew list with nationality flags adds a fun trivia element. Share a screenshot in a group chat and spark conversation about international cooperation in space.

## Best Practices for Space‑Aficionados  

1. **Sync your watch or phone with UTC** – The ISS’s data is in Coordinated Universal Time. Having your device set correctly ensures predictions match reality.  
2. **Use a clear, unobstructed view** – Even the best predictions are useless if trees, buildings, or weather block the line of sight.  
3. **Record your observations** – Many trackers allow you to log passes. Reviewing these logs can help you understand orbital patterns and refine future viewing strategies.  
4. **Stay Updated on Crew Changes** – The crew list updates regularly. Knowing who’s aboard can add context to your observations, especially during international missions.
